Research Gap: The Identification Barrier

Under existing EU law (Revised PLD 2024/2853) and Indian Tort, the victim cannot identify a proximate anchor. The AI Liability Directive was formally withdrawn in Oct 2025, leaving this void wide open.

Proposed Reform

Causal Unity

The Principal Chain Doctrine

Doctrine Core Principles

1. Unified Standing (Salomon v Salomon preserved)

Treats the delegation chain as co-defendants. The victim recovers in full from any co-defendant without identifying the micro-error.

2. Presumption of Linkage (Analogous to s.113B IEA)

Harm emerging from autonomous delegation is presumed linked to the initial delegator. Burden falls on developers to prove the agent acted outside scope.

Policy Impact

  • Remedy

    Solves the Causal Fog problem that leads to zero-recovery outcomes under the current EU AI Act role-based schema.

  • Safety

    Incentivizes mandatory Handshake Guardrails (budget caps, human-in-the-loop triggers) to avoid shared chain liability.
